Thousands of hospitals around the world are facing imminent ventilator shortages due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Multi-patient ventilation has been explored as a solution of last resort. Our goal is to provide hospitals and clinicians with a safe, reliable way to ventilate two patients with the same ventilator, should the need arise. The content of this website is open-source and may be used as needed within your hospital. We recommend that all providers follow their hospital system guidelines on multi-patient ventilation.
The COVID MultiVent System was developed through a collaboration between Dr. Arun Agrawal, pulmonary critical care fellow at the University of Missouri, and a group of medical students at the University of Pennsylvania with backgrounds in engineering. It requires no electronic components and is assembled from components that are readily available in most hospitals. The system allows providers to monitor the pressure and volume delivered to each patient and allows for peak inspiratory pressure (PIP), positive end expiratory pressure (PEEP), and inspiratory volume to be modulated independently for each patient.
We hope that solutions such as the COVID MultiVent will prove to be unnecessary in combating this pandemic. But the reality is that many ICUs will soon have to make hard choices about how to allocate their limited resources to address patient needs. We hope that you find the COVID MultiVent and affiliated information educational, useful, and implementable.
